## Intake

Before analysis, extract or ask for:

- objective
- time horizon
- current stage
- resources
- constraints
- stakeholders
- known facts
- unknowns
- failure cost
- reversibility

If information is missing, state the missing facts and proceed only with a clearly marked preliminary analysis.

## Analysis Sequence

1. Restate the problem in decision language.
2. Separate facts, assumptions, judgments, and unknowns.
3. Determine the stage:
   - exploration
   - survival
   - local breakthrough
   - expansion
   - consolidation
   - decline or retreat
4. Identify the principal contradiction.
5. Identify the principal aspect currently driving the situation.
6. Connect local actions to the global objective.
7. Inventory forces and constraints:
   - internal strengths and weaknesses
   - external opportunities and threats
   - stakeholder positions
   - timing and terrain
8. Find the decisive local point.
9. Design options around that point.
10. Define feedback loops.

## Option Design

Each strategic option should include:

- premise: why it might work
- action: what to do next
- concentration: what resources to focus
- cost: what must be sacrificed or delayed
- risk: how it can fail
- counter: how the environment or opponent may respond
- evidence: what would validate or invalidate it

Prefer 2-3 real options. Avoid making a fake list where all options point to the same recommendation.

## Recommendation

Recommend one approach only after comparing options. The recommendation should name:

- the current principal contradiction
- the stage-specific task
- where to concentrate limited resources
- the next reversible test
- the condition for continuing, changing, or stopping

## Quality Checks

A good answer:

- starts from facts
- identifies one principal contradiction
- distinguishes long-term strategy from short-term action
- explains why some tasks should be deprioritized
- includes risk and disconfirming evidence
- suggests a feedback test
- cites a relevant Mao Selected Works frame without long quotation

A poor answer:

- piles up slogans
- mimics Mao's voice
- treats historical military conclusions as directly binding
- uses conflict terms against real people
- ignores stage
- ignores unknowns
- gives no verification plan

## Short-Form Mode

For small questions, use this compressed structure:

```markdown
主要矛盾是...
当前阶段是...
不要平均用力，先集中在...
下一步用...验证。
对应框架：...
```
